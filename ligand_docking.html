Experiment 8

Molecular Docking and Interaction Analysis Between Ligand and Protein

View Aim & Objectives Read Principle

Aim & Objectives

Aim

To perform molecular docking simulations and analyze interactions between ligands and target proteins.

Objectives

  • Performing molecular docking using PyRx software.
  • Analysis of ligand protein interaction by PyMol.
  • To analyze binding poses and interaction patterns between ligand and protein.
  • To evaluate binding affinity and identify key interaction sites.

Principle

Molecular docking predicts the preferred orientation of a ligand when bound to a target protein. The process involves searching for optimal binding conformations by exploring the conformational space. Docking algorithms calculate binding energies and scores to rank potential binding poses. Interaction analysis identifies hydrogen bonds, hydrophobic interactions, and van der Waals forces between ligand and protein. This computational approach helps in understanding drug-protein interactions and aids in rational drug design by predicting binding affinities and identifying potential binding sites.

Output

Conclusion

The molecular docking experiment successfully demonstrated the prediction of ligand-protein binding conformations and interaction analysis. Through PyRx docking simulations, we identified optimal binding poses and calculated binding affinity scores to rank potential interactions. The interaction analysis using PyMOL revealed specific molecular interactions including hydrogen bonds, hydrophobic contacts, and van der Waals forces between the ligand and protein residues. This experiment emphasized the importance of molecular docking in understanding drug-protein interactions and rational drug design. The ability to predict binding modes and analyze interactions computationally provides valuable insights into the mechanism of action and helps in optimizing lead compounds. Molecular docking serves as a crucial tool in drug discovery, enabling the identification of promising drug candidates and guiding experimental validation studies.
