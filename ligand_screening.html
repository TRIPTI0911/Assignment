Experiment 7

Identification of Ligands, Screening of Ligands from PubChem and Virtual Screening by PyRx

View Aim & Objectives Read Principle

Aim & Objectives

Aim

To identify potential ligands from PubChem and perform virtual screening using PyRx.

Objectives

  • Identification and screening of ligand from PubChem database using Lipinski's rule of five.
  • Virtual screening of ligand by using PyRx software.

Principle

Ligand identification and virtual screening are computational techniques used in drug discovery. PubChem provides curated chemical structures along with physicochemical properties. Ligands are prepared and filtered based on drug-likeness criteria. PyRx integrates docking engines to screen multiple ligands against a target protein. Binding affinity scores predict the likelihood of interaction. Virtual screening reduces time and cost compared to experimental methods.

Output

Conclusion

The ligand screening experiment successfully demonstrated the identification and virtual screening of potential drug-like compounds from PubChem. Through the application of Lipinski's Rule of Five, we filtered ligands based on drug-likeness criteria including molecular weight, log P, hydrogen bond donors, and acceptors. The virtual screening using PyRx enabled us to evaluate multiple ligands against the target protein, ranking them based on binding affinity scores. This experiment highlighted the efficiency of computational screening methods in drug discovery, as they allow rapid evaluation of large compound libraries at a fraction of the cost and time required for experimental screening. The ability to identify and screen potential ligands computationally is essential for rational drug design and provides a valuable first step in the drug discovery pipeline, narrowing down candidates for further experimental validation.
